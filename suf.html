To understand about steady and unsteady flow first of all we have to know about velocity field.

Velocity Field

Velocity is a vector quantity, thus velocity filed is a vector field. We can represent it as:-

∨⃗=∨⃗(x,y,z,t)

It indicates the velocity of a fluid particle that is passing through the point x, y, z at time instant t, in the Eulerian sense. We can keep measuring the velocity at the same point or choose any other point x, y, z at the next time instant; the point x, y, z is not the ongoing position of an individual particle, but a point we choose to look at.

Steady Flow





If properties of a field do not change with time at any point, then the flow is termed as steady flow. Mathematically it can be stated as:

∂N∕∂t=0

where N represent any fluid property.Thus steady flow can be written as:

∂ρ∕∂t=0 or ρ=ρ(x,y,z)

and

∂∨⃗/∂t=0 or ∨⃗=∨⃗(x,y,z)

Properties may vary from one point to another, but they remain constant with time at each every point then the flow is Steady flow. Temporal accelaration is zero at any instant.

Example:-





Fluid flowing through pipes, nozzles, diffusers, and pumps is example of steady flow.

Unsteady Flow





If properties at a point vary with time then the flow is called unsteady or transient flow. Temporal accelaration is non-zero at some instant.

Example:-

Fluid flowing through tidal waves, running taps, rivers and ocean currents is example of unsteady flow.

Uniform Flow

If velocity is constant throughout the flow field i.e. independent of all space co-ordinates then the flow is called Uniform flow. Convective accelaration is zero.

Non-Uniform Flow

If velocity is inconsistenet throughout the flow field i.e. dependent on all space co-ordinates then the flow is called Non-Uniform flow. Convective accelaration is non-zero.
